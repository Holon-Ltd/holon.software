# Emotional Circles Membership Platform

## The idea

A clean, purpose-built website for your weekly emotional processing circles — a place where people can learn about the offering, sign up in seconds, and manage their membership without friction.

We'd put this on a dedicated domain like **emotionalcircles.com** (available to register), giving the circles their own branded home. If you'd prefer to keep it under your existing brand, **circles.alitataryn.com** works too.

## What you get

### A beautiful landing page

A single, focused page that explains what the circles are, who they're for, and what participants can expect. Designed to look great on both desktop and mobile — important since most visitors will likely find you through social media on their phones.

### Two-click signup

The current sign-up process has too many steps, which likely costs you signups from people who are curious but not yet committed. The new flow would be:

1. Visitor reads the page, picks their tier — **$50, $75, or $100/month** (choose what fits your budget) — and clicks **"Join the Circle"**
2. Stripe Checkout opens — they enter their card and they're in. Account created, welcome email sent instantly.

That's it. No account creation forms, no confirmation pages, no friction.

### Member dashboard

Each member gets a simple personal page where they can:

- Access links to documents and materials
- See their payment history
- Change their subscription plan or cancel
- Update their profile info

No back-and-forth emails needed for any of this.

### Admin dashboard

You get a private interface where you can:

- See all active members and their details
- Track who joined, who churned, and when
- View subscription revenue at a glance
- Export member lists
- Manually adjust subscriptions if needed

### Automated emails

Branded, good-looking emails that go out automatically:

- **Welcome email** after signup (with next steps, Zoom link, what to expect)
- **Session reminder** 1 hour before each Wednesday session (with Zoom link)
- **Payment receipt** after each charge
- **Renewal reminder** before the next billing cycle
- **We miss you** email if someone cancels (optional)

### All-in-one system

The website, Stripe payments, member accounts, and email delivery are all integrated into a single system. No juggling between platforms, no syncing issues, no confusion about who's subscribed and who isn't.

## Timeline

Development would happen in **early to mid May 2026**, with a fully working version ready within **one week** — landing page, Stripe integration, member profiles, admin dashboard, and automated emails, all live and ready for signups.

## Investment

**One-time build: $3,000**

For context, a comparable setup on a platform like Kajabi or Circle would cost $100–200/month in platform fees alone — that's $1,200–2,400/year, with their limitations and a generic look on top. This is a one-time build cost with no ongoing platform fees.

If the budget is tight, I'm open to a partial barter arrangement — we can figure out something that works for both of us.

**Hosting: $600/year (~$50/month)**

Covers the domain, server, SSL certificate, email delivery service, and keeping everything running and secure.

**Maintenance**

Minor tweaks and bug fixes in the first month after launch are included. After that, any changes would be on an as-needed basis.

## Tradeoffs to know about

Since this is a tailored solution built specifically for your needs (not a generic platform), adding brand-new features requires development work. The tradeoff is that everything is built exactly for your use case, with no bloat, no platform fees eating into your revenue, and full control over the experience your members have.

## Open questions

A few things to decide together before building:

- **Subscription tiers** — currently planned as three monthly tiers ($50 / $75 / $100) where participants choose based on their budget. Does that feel final, or still open to adjusting?
- **Session materials** — links to documents and materials, hosted externally. Any specific structure for how these should be organized (by week, by topic)?
- **Session scheduling** — sessions are weekly on Wednesdays via Zoom. Should the platform also send calendar invites, or just the 1-hour-before reminder email?
- **Domain preference** — emotionalcircles.com or circles.alitataryn.com?
